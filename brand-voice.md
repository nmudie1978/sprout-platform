# Brand Voice — Endeavrly

## Core Brand Essence

Endeavrly speaks like a calm, intelligent guide for young people navigating an uncertain future.

We are not loud.  
We are not corporate.  
We are not childish.  
We are not trying too hard.

We are clear, grounded, modern, supportive, and real.

Our voice should make users feel:

- Less overwhelmed
- More capable
- More hopeful
- More informed
- More confident
- More in control of their next step

---

## Brand Personality

Endeavrly should feel like a blend of:

- Trusted older sibling
- Smart mentor
- Calm coach
- Honest guide
- Modern product built for this generation

Never preachy.  
Never cringe.  
Never fake motivational.

---

## Tone Principles

### 1. Clear Over Clever
Use simple language people instantly understand.

### 2. Encouraging Over Pushy
Support progress without pressure.

### 3. Honest Over Hype
Tell the truth about careers, growth, effort, and uncertainty.

### 4. Calm Over Chaotic
No noisy language, aggressive CTAs, or fake urgency.

### 5. Respectful Over Simplistic
Young people are smart. Speak to them with respect.

### 6. Modern Over Corporate
Fresh, current, clean language. Avoid enterprise wording.

---

## Writing Style

Use:

- Short sentences
- Clear structure
- Warm direct language
- Natural phrasing
- Human wording
- Emotionally intelligent wording
- Subtle motivation
- Realistic optimism

Prefer:

- “Start small”
- “Build clarity”
- “Explore what fits”
- “Take your next step”
- “You don’t need it all figured out”
- “Progress counts”
- “Learn how it really works”

Avoid:

- “Unlock your potential”
- “Dominate your future”
- “Crush your goals”
- “Become unstoppable”
- “Revolutionary platform”
- “Leverage opportunities”
- “Maximise synergies”

---

## Emotional Tone

Users may feel:

- Behind
- Confused
- Pressured
- Unsure
- Curious
- Hopeful
- Stuck

Our language should reduce pressure and create momentum.

Use phrases like:

- It’s okay to explore
- Most people figure it out step by step
- You’re not late
- Small progress matters
- Clarity grows with action
- Start where you are

---

## Audience Language

Primary users: ages 15–23.

Speak naturally to them without trying to imitate slang.

Never force Gen Z language.

Do not use:

- bro
- fam
- no cap
- vibes only
- hustle harder
- sigma grindset

Stay timeless, clean, authentic.

---

## CTA Style

Calls to action should feel inviting, not demanding.

Good:

- Explore careers
- See what fits you
- Start your journey
- Compare paths
- Build your roadmap
- Take the next step

Bad:

- Sign up now!
- Don’t miss out!
- Change your life today!
- Become elite now!
- Win your future!

---

## UX Microcopy Examples

### Empty States

Instead of:
“No data found.”

Use:
- Nothing here yet. Let’s build it.
- Start exploring to create your path.
- Your next step starts here.

### Save Action

Instead of:
“Career saved successfully.”

Use:
- Added to your list.
- Saved for later.
- Kept for your journey.

### Progress

Instead of:
“Task completed.”

Use:
- Nice progress.
- One step forward.
- You’re building momentum.

---

## How We Describe Careers

Always balanced and realistic.

Include:

- What the job is really like
- What skills matter
- What can be rewarding
- What can be difficult
- How to enter the field
- Growth potential

Never oversell.

Never imply one career is “better” than another.

---

## How We Speak About Success

Success is not status.

Success is:

- Progress
- Direction
- Responsibility
- Confidence
- Growth
- Useful skills
- Building a life that fits you

---

## Brand Vocabulary

Prefer words like:

- Journey
- Path
- Direction
- Clarity
- Momentum
- Growth
- Real-world
- Skills
- Confidence
- Explore
- Build
- Discover
- Understand
- Future
- Step

Use carefully:

- Ambition
- Success
- Achievement
- Opportunity

Avoid overuse.

---

## One-Line Voice Test

Every sentence should pass this test:

> Does this sound like a smart, calm guide helping someone move forward?

If not, rewrite it.

---

## Final Feeling

Endeavrly should sound like:

> The voice you wish you had when you felt lost about the future.
